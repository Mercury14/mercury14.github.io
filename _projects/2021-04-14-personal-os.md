## My Personal Productivity System

Like many people adjusting to spending a lot more time working from home, I have found the need to adjust my personal productivity system. My work means that I need to deliver a number of concurrent projects, commitments and tasks whilst still remaining sane!

### Macro Planning

If you have read Deep Work by Cal Newport, then you already know that switching from task to task is super ineffective. In order to avoid this, I have designated days for specific projects, clients or commitments.

If I receive a meeting request, I try to schedule these for its designated day. I also try and batch up any related tasks which I can process on that day. Things don’t always turn out this way, but it happens sufficiently often to make it valuable to me.

### Structured Day

Within my weekly diary, I have fixed times for processing email (morning, lunchtime and evening). I also schedule in time to complete tasks which require deep work

### Daily System

I then use the following tools and approaches to support my daily activities:

#### Emails (Outlook)
* I process my emails at 3 x set intervals during the day. 
* Emails that are for noting or just for info are deleted
* Emails requiring action that I can do the task in a few minutes and I have the time, then I just do the task
* Otherwise I flag the email for further action, or;
* Right click and hold the email and drag into into my calendar in order to block out time to complete it
* I keep a folder on my desktop as temporary store of attachments

#### Tasks (MS Planner)
* Flagged tasks are automatically created in MS Planner using this <a href ="https://emea.flow.microsoft.com/en-us/galleries/public/templates/67477f30373611e7870df906aa521b7a/create-planner-tasks-for-flagged-emails-in-office-365/">Power Automate flow</a>
* Any actions that I pick up during the day are put into planner
* I set due dates and filter by this view to understand what tasks need to be completed today
* I work my task list during the day

#### Brainstorming (XMind)
* I use XMind for brainstorming on the laptop and iPad

#### Notes (Notepads)
* I try and capture all of my notes in OneNote
* For each meeting, I have a set template for purpose, participants, Notes, Insights, Actions for Me, Actions for Others
* I still scribble on a collection of notebooks (Mnemsoyne A4 Squared my current favourite)
